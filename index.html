Does GDP determine Living Standard and Happiness of the People?

Gross domestic product, also known as GDP, is a monetary measure of the market value of all the final goods and services produced and sold in a specific time period by countries. We might questioned, does the GDP of a country determine the living standard and the happiness of the people living in the country? This report will discover whether GDP is associated with the living standard of country and the happiness of people living in it.

In 2017, United States of America and China are both having the Top 2 Highest GDP among all country in the world which are approximately 19,000 billion dollars and 12,000 billion dollars. The country with GDP is Kiribati which has GDP of approximately 0.2 billion dollars.

Quality of Life Index(higher is better), is an attempt to measure the quality of life or well-being of a country. The value is an estimation of overall quality of life by using an empirical formula which takes into account climate index (higher is better), cost of living index (lower is better), health care index (higher is better), pollution index (lower is better), property price to income ratio (higher is better), purchasing power index (higher is better), safety index (higher is better), and lastly traffic commute time index (lower is better). The Top 3 Highest Quality of Life Index are Austria, Germany, and Denmark. However, the Top 2 Highest GDP countries which are United States, and China are ranked 7th, and 57th out of 66th countries that are involved in this survey. This indicates that the GDP of a country is not associated with the Quality of Life Index of a country.
